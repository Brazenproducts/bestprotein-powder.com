Where to Focus

  • Material grade — Higher-grade materials in protein powder last longer and perform better, even when designs look similar.
  • Build quality — Cheap protein powder fall apart fast. Look for solid construction and quality materials.
  • Return policy — Easy returns mean lower risk. Even well-reviewed protein powder might not suit your situation.
  • Dimensions matter — Read the actual measurements. "Large" means different things to different protein powder manufacturers.

Where Most Go Wrong

  • Skipping negative reviews — A 4.5-star product with durability complaints tells you more than a 5-star product with 8 reviews.
  • Ignoring the fine print — Some protein powder listings look great until you realize key parts are sold separately.
  • Going with the cheapest option — Budget protein powder cut corners. Spending 20% more often gets something that lasts 3x longer.
  • Blind brand loyalty — Your favorite brand might not make the best protein powder. Stay open to better options.
